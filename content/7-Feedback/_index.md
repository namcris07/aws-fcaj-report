> Below are my personal reflections, feedback, and constructive suggestions after completing 9 weeks in the **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** program. These insights are drawn from my hands-on experience, aimed at helping the FCAJ organizing team continuously refine the program for future cohorts.

### Overall Program Evaluation

**1. Learning & Working Environment**  
The FCAJ environment is highly dynamic, welcoming, and open. Speakers, mentors, and program admins were always ready to support and resolve complex technical questions (from IAM policies and VPC Peering to EKS Cluster setup and Argo CD GitOps), both during and outside formal hours. The spirit of peer collaboration across groups created an energetic and inspiring learning atmosphere.

**2. Support from Mentors & Team Admins**  
The mentorship team provided exceptionally dedicated guidance, focusing on imparting Architecture Thinking rather than just handing out answers. Mentors consistently encouraged students to practice hands-on, study official AWS documentation, and troubleshoot real issues independently (such as container port misconfigurations, ECR login token expiry, or pipeline breakages). Admins provided prompt assistance with resources, communication channels, and weekly progress tracking.

**3. Relevance to Academic Background**  
The real-world project **"DevSecOps CI/CD Factory on AWS EKS"** aligned perfectly with modern cloud engineering trends. The knowledge gained at FCAJ not only reinforced foundational networking and OS concepts from university coursework but also expanded into cutting-edge Cloud-Native, DevSecOps (Trivy, SonarQube, OWASP), GitOps (Argo CD), and Observability (CloudWatch) domains.

**4. Skill Development & Growth Opportunities**  
Throughout the 9 weeks, I matured not only in cloud technical skills but also in essential soft skills: root cause analysis for system failures, Agile/Scrum teamwork, professional slide deck presentations, and outcome-oriented engineering mindsets.

**5. Culture & Team Spirit**  
Team spirit was highlighted during project sprint deadlines. Team members readily offered cross-functional support (VPC infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, technical blogs, workshop lab guides), respecting diverse ideas and striving together to deliver high-quality project outcomes on deadline.

**6. Community Value & Networking**  
The program provided fantastic opportunities to attend Tech Meetups, Community Days, and Study Tours. Directly listening to AWS Solutions Architects, industry experts, and AWS User Group leaders expanded my real-world perspective and helped clearly map out my future cloud career path.

---

### Key Feedback & Responses (Q&A)

* **What did you find most satisfying during the program?**  
  👉 *What satisfied me most was gaining hands-on experience on real AWS infrastructure and enterprise-grade DevSecOps / GitOps tooling. Furthermore, the mentors' dedication to imparting "Job Zero Security" and "Build for Failure" mindsets completely transformed how I approach software architecture.*

* **What areas should the program improve for future cohorts?**  
  👉 *The program could introduce brief mid-week Office Hours or Q&A check-ins to help teams unblock technical issues faster. Additionally, providing standardized reference architecture templates early on would help new teams get up to speed quickly in the initial weeks.*

* **Would you recommend FCAJ to friends and peers? Why?**  
  👉 *I would 100% recommend FCAJ to my classmates and juniors. It is one of the most practical programs for bridging the gap between academic theory and enterprise requirements, simultaneously boosting technical competency and building strong community connections.*

---

### Suggestions & Future Expectations

* **Suggestions for Experience Enhancement:** Incorporate light team-bonding activities or minigames to boost cross-team interactions; expand sample hands-on labs covering AI Agents (Amazon Q, Security Agent) so students can stay ahead with the latest AI trends.
* **Future Intentions:** I would love to remain involved with the FCAJ community as a supporter/assistant mentor for upcoming cohorts, or participate in advanced research topics around Cloud Security and Kubernetes.
* **Closing Words:** A heartfelt thank you to the entire Organizing Team, Speakers, and Mentors of First Cloud AI Journey for creating such an inspiring, transformative, and valuable learning experience for students!
